# G900 force transformation theorem candidate 001

Status: theorem candidate

Statement

A transformation shadow exists if there is a replay-stable selector family whose registered traces repeatedly and persistently exhibit bounded lawful identity change through one or more of:

- state handoff
- identity replacement
- rule-boundary crossing
- branch conversion
- provenance-preserving transition

Definitions

Transformation shadow

A stable grammar of registered lawful identity change.

Weak shadow

Project shorthand for a transformation shadow.

Boundary

A transformation shadow is:

- not radioactive decay
- not weak interaction
- not W or Z bosons
- not physical weak force
- not body mutation
- not transport admission
- not a force renderer

Research question

Do existing G900 receipts and information transports exhibit replay-stable transformation signatures?

Success condition

At least one selector family survives replay and bounded perturbation tests while maintaining persistent bounded lawful identity-change receipts.
