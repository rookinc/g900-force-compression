\section{Tested-Family Uniqueness}

After verifying the return-cell lineage theorem, the next question was whether
homeward-alignment compression appears in other transport-labeled artifacts of
the apparatus.

A transport history surface inventory scanned 80 transport-labeled candidate
artifacts for constructible finite source and target supports. Only three
metric-constructible histories were found. All three belonged to the
return-cell packet lineage:

\begin{itemize}
  \item conserved packet scout 001,
  \item carrier-incidence packet scout 002,
  \item one-step information transport support audit 006.
\end{itemize}

All three shared the same slot signature

\[
\{3,6,9\} \rightarrow \{9,12,13\}.
\]

Thus, within the tested transport-labeled candidate family, only the
return-cell packet lineage is metric-constructible for homeward-alignment
compression testing.

This is not a global uniqueness theorem. It says only that, among the 80
transport-labeled artifacts tested in this phase, no non-return-cell transport
family exposed an explicit metric-constructible source-to-target slot history.
